Part 6 — Answers

  • a) Flat shading uses one constant shade per triangle. Gouraud computes lighting at vertices and interpolates colors across the face. Phong interpolates normals/positions and computes lighting per fragment. Flat shading can be implemented by using a single face normal per triangle (e.g., duplicate vertices per triangle with the same normal, or derive a face normal in the fragment shader and keep it constant). For highlights, Phong is usually best: Gouraud can miss highlights if they don’t hit near a vertex.
  • b) Directional light has the same direction everywhere (often no distance attenuation). Point light has a position; direction depends on the shaded point and intensity typically falls off with distance (often \(1/r^2\)).
  • c) Yes—mainly through the specular term, because the view direction depends on the eye position. Ambient/diffuse do not depend on the eye direction.
  • d) Setting the specular term to \((0,0,0)\) (or \(k_s=0\)) removes specular highlights; the material appears more matte.
  • e) Increasing \(s\) makes highlights smaller and sharper; decreasing it makes them wider and softer.
  • f) Lighting is computed in one consistent space. Here it’s effectively object space (equivalent to world space in this setup since \(M\) is identity).
